About Brown Ballroom!

Brown Ballroom has a rich history which spans decades at Brown. Founded in the early 1990s, Brown Ballroom is a group of students who are passionate about ballroom dance. In the club, we have Brown and RISD undergraduates, Brown graduate students, comunity members, and more. We aim to be a welcoming place where everyone, regardless of how long they have been dancing, can come and learn and improve!

Our Coaches

Kathy St. Jean

Co-Director of The Dancing Feeling, RI. She has been dancing since the age of 3 and has been teaching for many years! She has an extensive background in the performing arts (tap, jazz, ballet) and holds a degree in dance. Kathy has competed professionally in Latin, Standard, and American Smooth with numerous awards in both areas. She is a professional member of the Imperial Society of Teachers of Dance, North American Dance Teacher Association, Dance Teachers Club of Boston, Dance Educators, and USA Dance. In addition, she is a certified championship NDCA ballroom judge and a master level judge with the United Country Western Dance Council. In her classes, she emphasizes fun and good technique whether her students are bound for social dancing or professional competitive dancing. Kathy typically teaches our International Latin classes

Randy Deats

Co-Director of The Dancing Feeling, RI. Randy has been teaching ballroom dancing for over 30 years. He is a certified championship level NDCA judge and a certified master judge with the United Country Western Dance Council. He judges regional, national, and world-title events and often serves as Chairman of Judges and Master of Ceremonies. He is a member of the North American Teachers of Dance Association, and acts as an officer of the Dance Teachers' Club of Boston and the Imperial Society of Teachers of Dance. He has won numerous awards for his competitive dancing, his studio, his choreography, his PBS TV show "Dancing Disco," and his book. His experience, knowledge, and comfortable teaching style make people feel relaxed and confident so they can enjoy learning rapidly.

Istvan Cserven

Istvan enjoyed an extensive dance career in his native Hungary before moving to the United States. He held the amateur title of Hungarian Standard Champion, three-time Vice-Hungarian 10-Dance Champion, and was a member of the National Champion Latin Formation Team. For the next eleven years he coached competitive couples of all levels and ages, as well as the Hungarian Latin Youth Champions. He also served as the Vice President of the Hungarian Dance Sport Association for two years. Istvan joined the Fred Astaire Studios of New England in 1997 and has frequently been named Top Teacher at the Fred Astaire Tri-Star Championships, New England Dancesport Championships, and Colonial Classics. He is an accomplished Licentiate of the U.S. Imperial Society of Teachers of Dancing. About his passion and profession, he says, "I love to see people grow in all aspects of their life through developing their dance ability."
